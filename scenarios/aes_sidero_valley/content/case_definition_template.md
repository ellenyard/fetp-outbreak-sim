# Case Definition Template: Acute Encephalitis Syndrome (AES)

## WHO Standard Case Definition

### Suspected Case
A person of any age, at any time of year with acute onset of fever AND a change in mental status (including symptoms such as confusion, disorientation, coma, or inability to talk)

**AND/OR**

New onset of seizures (excluding simple febrile seizures)

### Probable Case
A suspected case PLUS cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) pleocytosis (white blood cells >5 cells/mmÂ³)

### Confirmed Case
A probable case with laboratory confirmation of Japanese Encephalitis:
- **Serology:** JE IgM antibody in CSF or serum
- **PCR:** JE virus RNA detected
- **Culture:** JE virus isolated from clinical specimen
- **Serology:** 4-fold rise in JE IgG antibody titer between acute and convalescent specimens

## Clinical Features to Document
- **Neurological:** Altered mental status, seizures, focal deficits, abnormal movements
- **Fever:** Onset date, duration, maximum temperature
- **Other symptoms:** Headache, vomiting, neck stiffness
- **Course:** Progression, complications, outcome

## Exclusions
Cases with alternative confirmed diagnoses:
- Bacterial meningitis (confirmed by culture)
- Cerebral malaria (confirmed by blood smear)
- Other viral encephalitides (confirmed by specific testing)
- Metabolic encephalopathy
- Traumatic brain injury
