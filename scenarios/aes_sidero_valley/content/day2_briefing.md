# Day 2: Hypothesis Testing and Data Collection

**Focus:** Testing hypotheses through targeted investigations and environmental assessments.

## Situation Update
- Active case search has identified additional suspected cases
- Geographic clustering around Nalu village confirmed
- Community concern is growing
- Local media beginning to report on the outbreak

## Your Tasks Today
1. **Test your hypotheses** - Design studies to investigate suspected risk factors
2. **Conduct environmental assessments** - Investigate potential exposure sites
3. **Interview cases and families** - Gather detailed exposure histories
4. **Collect environmental samples** - Water, vectors, animal specimens
5. **Coordinate with veterinary services** - Investigate animal health in affected areas
6. **Expand active case finding** - Search neighboring villages

## Investigation Priorities
- **Vector exposure:** Mosquito breeding sites, outdoor activities
- **Animal reservoirs:** Pig farming practices, proximity to pig pens
- **Environmental factors:** Rice paddies, standing water, wetlands
- **Host factors:** Vaccination status, age groups, occupational exposures

## One Health Considerations
This outbreak requires coordinated investigation across:
- **Human health:** Clinical surveillance, case investigation
- **Animal health:** Pig herd health, veterinary surveillance
- **Environmental health:** Vector surveillance, habitat assessment
